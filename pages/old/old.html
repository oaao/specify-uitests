You manage to find a real banger that gets you all fired up, so you dive into Spotify to spin up a related radio station based on the song you're obsessed with, hoping to keep the deafening beat going. Too often, you instead become a bystander to a sloppy and meager parade of tracks which are at best an approximation of what you actually wanted.

Specify solves this frustration by giving you more precision over musical attributes present in the songs recommended to you by the Spotify engine.

Feed it between 1 and 5 songs, artists, or genres to start things off, and freely tweak musical parameters on sliding scales: simple and definite things like tempo, key, and mode; aspects of tone and temperament like acoustic/electronic, instrumental/vocal, live/recorded, dancey/mellow, melancholy/cheery; and other things you might care about such as higher or lower popularity.

You'll receive a playlist of up to 100 tracks which you can play in-browser or save to your Spotify account - and find it that much easier to always scratch a sound itch whenever it pops up.
